    About me

    I was born in Madrid in 1987, and moved to a smaller city called Salamanca soon after. There I grew up, and studied the first three years of my Physics degree at the University of Salamanca. I then moved back to Madrid to take the specialization in Astrophysics at the Complutense University. I got my degree in Physics in early 2011, and at the time I was quite doubtful about what to do next. Luckily for me, I discovered the ESA trainee program at the European Space Astronomy Centre (ESAC) just in time. I got involved in a 6 month project to study star-forming regions with data from the Herschel Space Observatory. This was my very first contact with “real” research , and it gave me a great opportunity to work with state-of-the-art data and in an international environment, and a global idea of how science works. I loved it. After my traineeship, I got my master in Astrophysics (2011/2012) with a combined program at Complutense University and Autonoma University. At the same time, I joined the Herschel Science Center for one year as a astronomer tester for HIPE (the Herschel Interactive Processing Environment), the software used to process and analyze Herschel data.

    From late 2012 until today, I am a PhD student at ESAC under the supervision of Bruno Merín and Hervé Bouy, and funded by ESA . My research focuses on the characteristics and evolution of protoplanetary disks around young stars, to better understand how planets and planetary systems form and evolve.

    I have been passionate about literature, music and cinema since I was a child, and got later interested in photography too. I also write short stories, and have been very lucky to get some published in different contests. I have been doing outreach since I started my degree, either by directly talking to people or through my blog (in Spanish). I really enjoy traveling and discovering other cultures, something that my job involves quite often (luckily me!). Also sports have always been a vital part of my life, from basketball to fencing, rock climbing or scuba diving. And although I don’t have enough time for everything, I am really interested in other fields such as Philosophy and Biology. And of course, I would love to have more time for all these things!
